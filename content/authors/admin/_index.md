I am an Assistant Professor in the Theory & Practice in Teacher Education at the University of Tennessee at Knoxville. I earned my Ph.D. degree in Educational Technology from the University of Florida and a master's degree in Educational Technology from Seoul National University in South Korea. My research interests are the design and development of theoretically grounded AI technologies in education, learning analytics, future workforce development in AI, Data Science, and Computing. My goal is to provide engaging STEM learning experiences for all learners, regardless of their backgrounds, interests, and prior knowledge. I have extensive practical experience, having worked as an instructional designer for engineers at Hyundai Motor Company. I am passionate about teaching and have taught a wide range of students, from K-12 students to pre-service and in-service teachers, graduate students, and adult professionals. In my free time, I enjoy playing tennis, reading books, and working out.
